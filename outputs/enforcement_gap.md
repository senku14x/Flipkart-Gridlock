# ParkPulse: the enforcement gap

Where enforcement effort goes today vs where congestion impact actually is. Recorded violations stand in for effort (a violation is logged when a patrol is present); weighting each by its impact unit (obstruction x PCU x road utilization) shows how much real impact each slice of effort captures.

- The **4-5am sweep is 15.3% of effort but only 4.0% of congestion impact** (roads are empty, so a blockage barely matters).
- The **night window (12-5am) is 33.7% of effort and just 7.9% of impact**, about a third of logged enforcement, captured while congestion is near zero.
- The **peak-exposure hours are the reverse**: a smaller share of effort, the majority of the impact.

| Time window | Share of effort | Share of impact |
|---|--:|--:|
| Pre-dawn sweep (4-5am) | 15.3% | 4.0% |
| Night (12-5am) | 33.7% | 7.9% |
| Peak-exposure hours | 28.8% | 50.1% |

ParkPulse redirects effort from the low-impact night window to the peak-hour chokepoints where impact concentrates. The hour itself is enforcement-confounded, so we prioritize by exposure-weighted impact, not by the recorded violation hour.
